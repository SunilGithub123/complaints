3. Stakeholders & User Roles
For each role, define:
Who are they? (e.g., Consumer, Field Technician, Admin)
Consumers: Individuals who will submit complaints regarding electricity issues.
Field technicians: Professionals responsible for addressing and resolving complaints in the field.
Admins: System administrators who manage the system and ensure compliance with regulations.
Engineers: Professionals who analyze complaints, and assign them to field technicians based on their expertise.

What can they do? (permissions per role)
Consumers: Submit complaints, track complaint status, receive updates, and provide feedback on resolution.
Field technicians: View assigned complaints, update complaint status, communicate with consumers, and provide resolution details
Admins: Manage user accounts, oversee complaint resolution process, generate reports, and ensure compliance with regulations.
Engineers: Analyze complaints, assign them to field technicians based on their expertise, and monitor the resolution process.

How will they access the system? (web portal, mobile, SMS, IVR?)
web portal, mobile

4. Functional Requirements
4.1 Consumer Registration & Authentication
- Users can register Consumer id, mobile number and password.
- Users can login using Consumer id as id and password.


4.2 Complaint Creation:
- Users can submit complaints enven when they are not logged in(guest mode), but they will need to provide their consumer id and mobile number for verification.
- Users can submit complaints through a web portal or mobile app.
- One user can submit multiple complaints.
- Complaints can be categorized by severity.
- User can add images, description and location of the complaint.
- Categories like power outage, low voltage, transformer fault,other etc. will be available for users to select while submitting complaints.
- we will add autodetecting of location/address of the user later
- SLA will be of 24 hours initially and that can be changed later by admin.

4.3 Complaint management and tracking:
- Engineers can analyze complaints and assign them to field technicians based on their expertise.
- Admins can manage complaints, assign them to field technicians, and generate reports.
- Field technicians can view and update the status of assigned complaints.
- Users can track the status of their complaints in real-time, and can cancel their complaints.
- Users receive notifications about updates on their complaints.

4.4 Complaint Resolution & Closure
- Field technicians can resolve complaints and provide resolution details.
- Admins can monitor the resolution process and ensure compliance with regulations.
- Users can provide feedback on the resolution process.
- Complaints are closed when the resolution is complete.

4.6 Notifications & Alerts
- Users will receive notifications about updates on their complaints.
- Admins and Engineers will receive alerts about new complaints and updates.
- Users can opt-out of notifications.



Non-functional Requirements:
- Performance: There can be 1 million consumers, and may be 10000 complaints per day
- Scalability: The system should be able to handle a large number of users and complaints.
- Security: The system should ensure the security of user data and prevent unauthorized access.
- Usability: The system should be user-friendly and easy to navigate for all user roles.
- Reliability: The system should be reliable and available at all times, with minimal downtime.
- Accessibility: Language/multilingual support for English, Hindi, Marathi.

5. Integration Requirements

- Integration: System should integrate with existing systems and services for consumer data.
